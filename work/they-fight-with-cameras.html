Film . Art Direction

They Fight With Cameras

Client

Daedalus Productions

Role

Art Director

Year

2025 — Present

Status

In Production

The Challenge

A documentary that needed a visual language as unflinching as its subjects.

They Fight With Cameras tells the story of war photographers who shaped how the world sees conflict. The film demanded an art direction that honoured the gravity of its subject without becoming sentimental or overwrought.

Daedalus Productions approached me to define the visual identity of the campaign — from title design to promotional materials, festival submissions, and the broader narrative architecture that would carry the documentary from screen to audience.

The Approach

Restraint as a form of respect.

The visual system builds on contrasts: silence against noise, stillness against chaos. We developed a typographic-led identity using stark black-and-white photography, precise editorial framing, and a restrained colour palette that lets the subject matter speak.

Every design decision was guided by one principle: the photographers are the heroes, not the design. The visual language serves the story — never competes with it.

The best art direction disappears. It creates conditions for the story to land.

The Outcome

A visual system built for longevity.

The campaign identity has been adopted across all touchpoints — title sequences, press kits, festival submissions, social media, and merchandise. It provides a cohesive visual backbone that adapts to each format while maintaining the film's emotional integrity.

As the documentary moves through production and into distribution, the identity system continues to evolve — always guided by the same principles of editorial restraint and narrative clarity.
