The Challenge

The first project where the Brand Sprint method was shaped in practice.

Steen Digital, led by Philip Steen, was the first case where I applied and refined my Brand Sprint method end-to-end. Philip started from scratch, and the goal was to create a clear strategic and visual baseline that could support growth without losing clarity.

This case became the foundation for how I later ran the method with Radiologie Ahrensburg and LM Alltagshilfe.

The Approach

Build the method on a real business, not in theory.

The sprint focused on translating Steen Digital's practical, no-buzzword positioning into a coherent brand system. Instead of abstract strategy decks, the work was structured for direct use across communication and digital touchpoints.

My work brought structure, shape, and confidence across strategy, identity, and digital expression. By testing the method on a real operating business, we built a repeatable process that later became the blueprint for the next Brand Sprint cases.

Steen Digital was the proving ground: this is where the Brand Sprint method became practical, repeatable, and real.

The Outcome

A working method with a clear lineage.

The outcome was more than one case delivery: it established the working basis of my Brand Sprint method and delivered a full stack from strategy and positioning to identity, website, pitch deck, and templates. The same strategic structure and decision logic were then carried into Radiologie Ahrensburg and LM Alltagshilfe.

Philip and I still collaborate regularly on projects, and this case remains the reference point for that ongoing partnership.
